Desk and bias lighting for gaming setups

Bias lighting behind a monitor measurably reduces eye strain, separate from whatever aesthetic value RGB adds.

Best budget pick: RGBIC-style strip lights — ~£15-£28

Enough for a bias-lighting effect behind a monitor without turning the whole room into a light show — useful in a small room where a fully-lit RGB setup can feel overwhelming rather than atmospheric.

Best mid-range: Govee Strip Light 2 Pro — ~£100

Wireless control, wide colour range, syncs with Corsair iCUE if the rest of the setup is Corsair gear. A "once you know you want this hobby" purchase, not a starting recommendation.

The actual useful bit, regardless of budget

Bias lighting behind the monitor at roughly 300-500 lumens per metre measurably reduces eye strain by softening the contrast between a bright screen and a dark room.
